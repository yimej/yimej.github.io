TIL DEATH DO US CHART: A predictive analysis on marital status

Click image to view project.

The final project of the GWU Data Analytics Boot Camp, this deep-dive study used machine learning to predict marital status from live events recorded by the National Longitudinal Survey of Youth 1997.

The objective of this project was to utilize any to all skills that were covered in the program to create a comprehensive analysis and series of visualizations.

The most fascinating aspect of this project was the breadth and depth of the data set. The survey followed over 8,000 participants from their teens through their thirties, asking hundreds of questions and recording how responses changed over time. The specific interview question used in this study was "Respondent's marital status in this month in [1994-2016]," which was recorded every month after the subject’s fourteenth birthday.

The study confirmed that people are hard to predict. Single variables did not strongly correlate with marital status. However, combining variables increased the accuracy of predictions and the utility of the model.

Made with: Python, pandas, NumPy, JSON, JavaScript, D3, Plotly, Machine Learning, HTML5/CSS, Bootstrap

Source: National Longitudinal Survey of Youth 1997

WINE REVIEW ANALYSIS

The first of three GWU Data Analytic Boot Camp projects, this is a simple analysis of wine reviews from Kaggle.

The objective of this analysis was to assess whether wine quality correlates with (1) price (2) the country of origin.

After selecting eight wines (Pinot Noir, Red Blend, Merlot, Sangiovese, White Blend, Riesling, Chardonnay, Sauvignon Blanc) from a database of over 150,000 wines, the project group members utilized Python to explore the selected wines' various relationships among rating, price, and country of origin.

Made with: Python, pandas, MatPlotLib, NumPy

Source: Kaggle
